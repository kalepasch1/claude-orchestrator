# W2a-r — War-room CADE rosters: full standing expert panel + party advocates as direction-setters

Operator direction 2026-07-09. Amends W2a in PROMPT-revisions-cade-warroom-r2 (update decomposed tasks per dedup). Target: Tomorrow war rooms; same pattern applies to any CADE surface that currently seats only party personas.

**Requirement:** every war-room determination seats the FULL standing expert roster for all relevant domains (per CADE_IMPLEMENTATION_HANDOFF §3.1 — legal authorities, finance authorities, the interdisciplinary mosaic, adversary archetypes, reviewers), selected per issue via `filterByCompetence`/`selectPanel`, IN ADDITION to the party-position personas. The party personas (company advocate, counterparty model) **dictate direction and objectives**; the expert panel's super-intelligence determines how those objectives are best achieved.

- **Roster composition per contested issue**: (a) company advocate persona (from the user's Position Profile — sets the objective function, non-negotiable constraints, risk appetite); (b) counterparty model persona (from scouting data); (c) domain authorities + mosaic disciplines + adversaries + reviewers from the standing roster, panel-selected by issue competence requirements. Party advocates are NOT authorities in the panel math — they define what winning means; the panel determines the winning path.
- **Bias-cost receipt**: when the panel's optimal path diverges from the advocate's preferred path, quantify the divergence ("your preferred stance on the default trigger costs an expected $86k vs the panel path") and show it on the W2d option card. The user still steers — but sees the price of steering. This is the honest version of "guided by the super-intelligence": direction is theirs, the cost of direction is visible.
- **Shared kernel persona registry**: persona definitions + calibration/reliability scores live once at kernel level and are consumed by Apparently, Smarter, and Tomorrow rosters (no per-repo divergent copies) — calibration learned in one product compounds across all (extends §3.4 flywheel portfolio-wide).
- **Budget guard**: panel size laddered by clause materiality (B15/materiality policy G15); standing-roster cache (§6.2) reused across similar clauses/rooms; per-room CADE budget with G13 allocation. No room may be blocked by determination cost — degrade to cached standing positions + flag, never stall.
- Proof: panel-composition test (fixture issue seats authorities+mosaic+adversary+reviewer+both party personas); bias-cost receipt render test; registry single-source test; budget degradation test.
