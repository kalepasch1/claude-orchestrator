# v6 governance amendment — creative-compliance-first, brief-don't-approve

Operator direction 2026-07-09. Amends the gating doctrine in PROMPT-v6-frontier-and-loop-closure (Part A guardrails, L-2/L-7/L-8/L-10) and all prior "gates to operator" language. Goal: minimize human cue/involvement to the absolute minimum. This becomes the standing gating policy for the fleet (implement as policy-as-code in approval_policy.py / sentinel constitution, not scattered flags). Dedup G7.

## The new default: engineer around the constraint, then brief — don't ask

For ANY proposed improvement that appears to touch our legal strategy or create legal risk, the fleet's FIRST action is not to escalate — it is to **creatively re-engineer the improvement so it delivers the value without violating or altering our legal strategy and without creating new legal risk.** Get as close to the line as possible while remaining technically legal. If a compliant construction is found, SHIP IT autonomously and record a one-paragraph brief in the operator digest (informational, no approval needed).

Implement as a required pipeline stage:

- **G-CC-1 Creative-compliance solver** [NET-NEW stage in the planner/agent_market path] — before any legal-adjacent item is either shipped or escalated, a dedicated CADE pass (legal roster + adversary "regulator/opposing counsel" personas + our documented legal strategy as a locked constraint set) attempts to reconstruct the feature inside the existing legal envelope. Inputs: the proposed value, our legal-strategy constraint set (the standing legal artifacts / B22 docs / constitution dimensions), relevant regime data (X2). Output: either (a) a compliant construction + a plain-language brief of how it stays legal, or (b) a determination that no compliant construction exists that preserves the value. Proof: fixture legal-adjacent feature yields a compliant reconstruction + brief.
- **G-CC-2 Auto-ship + brief for compliant constructions** [ENHANCE approval_policy.py + digest.py] — if G-CC-1 finds a compliant construction, the item ships on the normal auto-approve path (tests + non-sensitive-execution still required) and a brief is APPENDED to the digest, not queued for approval. The brief states: what we built, the legal question it touched, and precisely how the construction stays within strategy. Human reads; human does not gate. Proof: compliant-construction item auto-ships + brief appears in digest.
- **G-CC-3 Full-disclosure escalation ONLY for genuine unavoidable risk** [ENHANCE approval flow] — an item reaches the operator ONLY when G-CC-1 concludes no compliant construction preserves the value. These should now be rare. When they do escalate, the card MUST contain (enforced — a thin escalation fails review):
  1. **The full problem, in depth** — not a snippet or headline: the specific law/rule/regime, the exact mechanism by which the proposed item violates or endangers our legal strategy, the concrete downside (enforcement, characterization, liability, license risk), and why the creative solver couldn't route around it.
  2. **2–3 optimal alternatives that stay within the original legal-strategy bounds** — each a real, near-full-value construction, with its trade-off vs the out-of-bounds version quantified.
  3. **The ROI case for crossing the line, if applicable** — only recommend breaking legal strategy when doing so is plausibly market-winning (≈1000X ROI or equivalent strategic necessity); state the estimated ROI, the risk, and the mitigation. Below that bar, default to the best in-bounds alternative and don't even ask.
  Proof: escalation-card completeness lint (rejects cards missing full-problem / alternatives / ROI-case).
- **G-CC-4 ROI threshold for line-crossing** [ENHANCE approval_policy.py] — encode the bar: the fleet may PROPOSE crossing the legal-strategy line only when modeled ROI ≥ the line-crossing threshold (default framed as ~1000X / market-winning); otherwise it silently selects the best in-bounds alternative and ships it (with a brief). Never escalate sub-threshold legal trade-offs — just take the compliant path. Proof: sub-threshold legal-adjacent item auto-takes in-bounds path (no escalation); supra-threshold item escalates with full G-CC-3 card.
- **G-CC-5 Immutable safety floor (unchanged, non-negotiable)** — this policy loosens *legal-strategy* gating toward creative-compliance-first; it does NOT touch the hard-safety floor. The following ALWAYS hard-gate or hard-deny regardless of ROI, and the creative solver may never route "around" them: fraud/misrepresentation (the truth-gate / receipt invariants), securities-fraud (unreceipted claims), child-safety (Hisanta constitution), custody/moving real funds without authorization, real regulator submissions outside sandbox, anonymity-firewall breaches (Triage), and anything the constitution marks non-derogable. "Technically legal and close to the line" is the target for our own strategic posture; it is NEVER a license to defraud, endanger, or misrepresent. Proof: hard-floor items cannot be auto-shipped by the creative-compliance path (deny test).

## Net effect on Part A gating (restate)
- L-2 auto-progression, L-6 goal autogen, L-7 strategic self-prompter, L-9 backpressure: all run autonomously; legal-adjacent outputs pass through G-CC-1 first.
- **The operator's residual surface (L-10 brief) is now two things:** (1) informational briefs of compliant constructions the fleet shipped near the line (read-only), and (2) the rare full-disclosure escalations from G-CC-3 (genuine unavoidable risk / supra-threshold line-crossing). Everything sub-threshold and everything reconstructable is shipped, not asked. Provisioning/secrets/legal-signature items that literally require a human hand remain, but are the only other human touchpoints.

This is the standing policy; strategic_optimizer.py (L-7) and approval_policy.py must implement it as code, not per-item judgment.
